4.30 Asset Identity Policy

4.30.1 Purpose

The Asset Identity Policy defines the constitutional requirements for establishing, preserving, and verifying the identity of every asset within the Berry Platform™.Identity is the foundation of digital truth — without it, provenance collapses, lineage breaks, and integrity becomes unverifiable.

This policy ensures that every asset has a permanent, immutable, cryptographically anchored identity that cannot be rewritten, reassigned, or forged.

4.30.2 Constitutional Requirements

1. Immutable IdentityHash

Every asset must possess a unique, immutable identityHash generated at creation.This hash:

is derived from canonical identity fields

cannot be altered after creation

serves as the root of all provenance, lineage, and integrity structures

is globally unique across all worlds and timelines

If the identityHash changes, the asset is no longer the same asset.

2. Canonical Identity Fields

Identity is defined by a set of canonical fields, including:

asset type

creation timestamp

creator identity

world of origin

initial metadata schema

initial integrityHash

These fields form the identity substrate, which is hashed to produce the identityHash.

3. Identity Cannot Be Reassigned

An asset’s identity:

cannot be transferred

cannot be merged with another asset

cannot be split into multiple assets

cannot be overwritten by world‑level rules

Identity is constitutional — not editorial.

4. Identity Must Be Verifiable

Any system interacting with an asset must be able to:

recompute the identityHash

verify canonical identity fields

confirm that the asset has not been replaced or substituted

detect identity drift or tampering

Identity verification is mandatory at all trust boundaries.

4.30.3 Identity Creation Rules

1. Identity is created at the moment of asset genesis

Identity is established before any provenance, metadata, or world‑level rules are applied.

2. Identity creation must be deterministic

Given the same canonical fields, the identityHash must always be identical.

3. Identity creation must be logged

The identity creation event becomes the first entry in the asset’s provenance chain.

4.30.4 Identity Preservation

Identity must remain stable across:

world migrations

metadata updates

custody transfers

version changes

resurrection events

cross‑world interactions

compliance transformations

export/import operations

Identity is the one thing that never changes.

4.30.5 Identity Violations

Any attempt to:

alter identityHash

rewrite canonical identity fields

substitute an asset with another

merge or split identity

regenerate identity outside constitutional rules

…constitutes an identity violation.

Identity violations must:

be logged

trigger zero‑trust enforcement

be escalated to Platform Governance

invalidate any dependent provenance or lineage claims

Identity violations are treated as existential threats to digital truth.

4.30.6 Constitutional Principle

Identity is the root of truth.If identity fails, everything fails.
