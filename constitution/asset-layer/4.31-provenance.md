4.31 Asset Provenance Policy

4.31.1 Purpose

The Asset Provenance Policy defines how the complete, continuous, and tamper‑evident history of an asset is recorded, preserved, and verified within the Berry Platform™.

Provenance is the narrative spine of an asset — the constitutional record of everything that has ever happened to it.Without provenance, identity is meaningless, lineage is impossible, and integrity cannot be trusted.

This policy ensures that provenance is:

complete

continuous

immutable

reconstructable

cryptographically anchored

zero‑trust verifiable

4.31.2 Constitutional Requirements

1. Continuous Provenance Chain

Every asset must maintain a continuous, unbroken provenance chain beginning at identity creation.

Each event in the chain must include:

event type

timestamp

actor identity

world context

previous integrityHash

new integrityHash

event metadata

cryptographic signature

No gaps.No silent edits.No retroactive insertions.

2. Immutable Provenance Entries

Once written, a provenance entry:

cannot be modified

cannot be deleted

cannot be reordered

cannot be replaced

Any attempt to alter provenance is a constitutional violation.

3. Cryptographic Anchoring

Each provenance entry must:

be hashed

reference the previous entry’s hash

update the asset’s integrityHash

be verifiable independently of world‑level systems

This creates a hash‑chained, tamper‑evident history.

4. Provenance Must Be World‑Agnostic

Provenance must remain valid across:

world migrations

cross‑world interactions

multiverse transitions

external system integrations

Worlds may add context, but they cannot alter canonical provenance.

4.31.3 Provenance Event Types

Provenance must record, at minimum:

identity creation

metadata updates

custody transfers

world migrations

version changes

compliance transformations

reconstruction events

resurrection events

deprecation actions

arbitration decisions

integrity violations

security incidents

Every meaningful change to an asset must be logged.

4.31.4 Provenance Verification

Any system interacting with an asset must be able to:

recompute the provenance chain

verify all hashes

detect missing or altered entries

confirm continuity

validate signatures

reconstruct the asset’s full history

Verification is mandatory at all trust boundaries.

4.31.5 Provenance Preservation

Provenance must survive:

world shutdowns

system failures

catastrophic data loss

cross‑system exports

compliance redactions

resurrection events

Provenance is the constitutional memory of the asset.

4.31.6 Provenance Violations

Any attempt to:

remove provenance entries

rewrite history

forge events

break continuity

bypass logging

suppress integrity updates

…constitutes a provenance violation.

Violations must:

be logged

trigger zero‑trust enforcement

invalidate dependent lineage claims

escalate to Platform Governance

Provenance violations are treated as attempts to rewrite truth.

4.31.7 Constitutional Principle

Provenance is the story of truth.If provenance breaks, truth collapses.
