4.34 Asset Custody & Ownership Policy

4.34.1 Purpose

The Asset Custody & Ownership Policy defines how control, possession, and responsibility for an asset are established, transferred, and verified within the Berry Platform™.

Custody is the constitutional record of who holds an asset at any moment in time.It is not a suggestion, not a world‑level rule, and not a UI concept — it is a binding, cryptographically anchored truth.

Custody ensures:

assets cannot be silently transferred

ownership cannot be forged

possession cannot be disputed

worlds cannot override canonical control

every transfer is logged, signed, and verifiable

Custody is the constitutional safeguard against ambiguity.

4.34.2 Constitutional Requirements

1. Canonical Custody Record

Every asset must maintain a canonical custody record that includes:

current custodian identity

previous custodian identity

custody transfer timestamp

transfer event metadata

cryptographic signatures

updated integrityHash

This record must be:

immutable

continuous

world‑agnostic

independently verifiable

Custody is not a world‑level concept — it is constitutional.

2. Custody Must Be Explicit

Custody cannot change implicitly.

A custody transfer requires:

explicit action

explicit logging

explicit signatures

explicit integrity updates

No silent transfers.No inferred ownership.No “assumed control.”

3. Custody Must Be Verifiable

Any system interacting with an asset must be able to:

verify the current custodian

verify the full custody chain

detect forged or missing transfers

confirm continuity

validate signatures

recompute integrity

Custody must be provable without trusting any world or actor.

4.34.3 Custody Transfer Rules

A custody transfer must:

reference the previous custodian

specify the new custodian

include a timestamp

include both parties’ signatures (where applicable)

update the integrityHash

be added to the provenance chain

be added to the custody chain

A custody transfer is not complete until all constitutional requirements are met.

4.34.4 Custody vs. Ownership

Custody and ownership are related but distinct:

Custody = who currently holds the asset

Ownership = who has constitutional rights over the asset

Ownership may be:

individual

shared

delegated

institutional

world‑level (in special cases)

Ownership changes must follow the same constitutional rules as custody transfers.

4.34.5 Custody Preservation

Custody must remain intact across:

world migrations

cross‑world interactions

multiverse transitions

compliance transformations

resurrection events

disaster recovery

export/import operations

Custody is not local — it is canonical.

4.34.6 Custody Violations

Any attempt to:

transfer custody without logging

forge custodian identity

bypass signatures

suppress custody events

rewrite custody history

create ambiguous ownership

override custody at the world level

…constitutes a custody violation.

Violations must:

be logged

trigger zero‑trust enforcement

invalidate dependent provenance and lineage claims

escalate to Platform Governance

Custody violations are treated as attempts to steal or falsify control.

4.34.7 Constitutional Principle

Custody is the truth of control.If custody is unclear, control is illegitimate.
