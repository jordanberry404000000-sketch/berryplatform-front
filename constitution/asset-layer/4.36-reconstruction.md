4.36 Asset Reconstruction & Canonical Restoration Policy

4.36.1 Purpose

The Asset Reconstruction & Canonical Restoration Policy defines how the Berry Platform™ restores assets after corruption, loss, tampering, or catastrophic failure — without breaking identity, provenance, lineage, or integrity.

Reconstruction is not recovery.Reconstruction is forensic restoration — the constitutional ability to rebuild an asset from its canonical truth, even when the original data is damaged or missing.

This is one of the core guarantees that makes Berry a zero‑trust, disaster‑resilient institution.

4.36.2 Constitutional Requirements

1. Reconstruction Must Preserve Identity

Reconstruction must:

maintain the original identityHash

preserve canonical identity fields

never generate a new identity

never create a replacement asset

If identity changes, it is not reconstruction — it is a violation.

2. Reconstruction Must Preserve Provenance

Reconstruction must:

replay the full provenance chain

verify all event hashes

detect missing or altered entries

restore continuity

update integrityHash accordingly

Provenance is the blueprint for reconstruction.

3. Reconstruction Must Preserve Lineage

Reconstruction must:

maintain all ancestors

maintain all descendants

maintain forks and merges

maintain world migrations

maintain resurrection ancestry

Lineage is the genetic memory of the asset.

4. Reconstruction Must Be Deterministic

Given the same:

identity

provenance

lineage

metadata schema

…reconstruction must always produce the same canonical state.

No nondeterministic behavior.No world‑specific variations.

4.36.3 Reconstruction Triggers

Reconstruction may be triggered when:

data corruption occurs

metadata is lost

world systems fail

storage is compromised

compliance redaction requires representational reconstruction

resurrection events occur

cross‑world migrations require canonical rehydration

integrity violations are detected

Reconstruction is constitutional, not optional.

4.36.4 Reconstruction Process

A valid reconstruction must:

Verify identity

Rebuild provenance from the first event

Rebuild lineage from canonical ancestry

Recompute integrityHash at each step

Rehydrate metadata according to schema

Restore custody state

Validate signatures

Produce a canonical, verifiable asset state

Reconstruction is a full replay of truth, not a patch.

4.36.5 Partial Reconstruction

If some data is missing:

missing metadata must be marked as unknown

missing provenance entries must be flagged as gaps

missing lineage branches must be flagged as incomplete

integrityHash must reflect partial reconstruction

the asset must remain verifiable despite incompleteness

Berry never fabricates truth — it represents uncertainty explicitly.

4.36.6 Reconstruction Violations

Any attempt to:

reconstruct without provenance

fabricate missing history

alter identity

collapse lineage

bypass integrity recomputation

hide reconstruction events

produce nondeterministic results

…constitutes a reconstruction violation.

Violations must:

be logged

trigger zero‑trust enforcement

escalate to Platform Governance

invalidate dependent claims

Reconstruction violations are treated as attempts to forge reality.

4.36.7 Constitutional Principle

Reconstruction is the restoration of truth.If reconstruction is untrustworthy, truth cannot survive catastrophe.
