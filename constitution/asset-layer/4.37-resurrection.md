4.37 Asset Revival, Resurrection & Canonical Restoration Policy

4.37.1 Purpose

The Asset Revival, Resurrection & Canonical Restoration Policy defines how the Berry Platform™ restores assets that have been lost, destroyed, corrupted beyond reconstruction, or otherwise rendered non‑functional — while preserving identity, provenance, lineage, and constitutional truth.

Resurrection is not duplication.Resurrection is not recreation.Resurrection is the constitutional restoration of an asset’s existence when its canonical state can no longer be recovered through standard reconstruction.

This is one of the most powerful guarantees in the Berry Platform™.

4.37.2 Constitutional Requirements

1. Resurrection Must Preserve Identity

A resurrected asset must:

retain the original identityHash

retain canonical identity fields

never generate a new identity

never be treated as a new asset

If identity changes, it is not resurrection — it is a violation.

2. Resurrection Must Preserve Provenance

Resurrection must:

preserve the full provenance chain

mark missing entries as irrecoverable

maintain continuity

update integrityHash accordingly

log the resurrection event as a provenance entry

Resurrection does not erase history — it acknowledges loss.

3. Resurrection Must Preserve Lineage

A resurrected asset must:

maintain all ancestors

maintain all descendants

maintain forks and merges

maintain world migrations

maintain deprecation ancestry

Lineage is the anchor that prevents resurrection from becoming replacement.

4. Resurrection Must Be Explicit

Resurrection cannot occur implicitly.

A resurrection event must:

be intentionally triggered

be logged

be cryptographically signed

update integrityHash

be world‑agnostic

be independently verifiable

Resurrection is a constitutional act, not a system convenience.

4.37.3 Resurrection Triggers

Resurrection may be invoked when:

all canonical data is lost

reconstruction is impossible

metadata is unrecoverable

provenance is partially missing

world systems have collapsed

catastrophic corruption has occurred

compliance redaction requires representational resurrection

multiverse transitions require canonical rehydration

Resurrection is the last line of defense for digital truth.

4.37.4 Resurrection Process

A valid resurrection must:

Verify identity

Restore canonical identity fields

Rebuild provenance as fully as possible

Mark missing history explicitly

Rebuild lineage from ancestry

Rehydrate metadata according to schema

Restore custody state

Recompute integrityHash

Log the resurrection event

Resurrection is not reconstruction — it is constitutional re‑establishment.

4.37.5 Representational Resurrection

When full restoration is impossible:

missing metadata is marked as unknown

missing provenance is marked as irrecoverable

missing lineage branches are marked as incomplete

the asset remains canonical despite incompleteness

integrityHash reflects representational truth

Berry never fabricates truth — it represents uncertainty faithfully.

4.37.6 Resurrection Violations

Any attempt to:

resurrect without identity

fabricate missing history

collapse lineage

bypass integrity recomputation

hide resurrection events

create a new identity under the guise of resurrection

replace an asset instead of restoring it

…constitutes a resurrection violation.

Violations must:

be logged

trigger zero‑trust enforcement

escalate to Platform Governance

invalidate dependent claims

Resurrection violations are treated as attempts to forge existence.

4.37.7 Constitutional Principle

Resurrection is the return of truth.If resurrection is unbounded, truth becomes replaceable.
