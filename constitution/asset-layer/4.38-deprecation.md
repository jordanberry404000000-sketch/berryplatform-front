4.38 Asset Deprecation, Sunset & End‑of‑Life Policy

4.38.1 Purpose

The Asset Deprecation, Sunset & End‑of‑Life Policy defines how assets transition out of active use while preserving their identity, history, and constitutional truth.

Deprecation is not destruction.Sunset is not disappearance.End‑of‑life is not erasure.

This policy ensures that assets can be retired without compromising provenance, lineage, integrity, or canonical truth.

4.38.2 Constitutional Requirements

1. Deprecation Must Be Explicit

An asset cannot be deprecated implicitly or silently.

A deprecation event must:

be intentionally triggered

be logged in provenance

update the integrityHash

be cryptographically signed

be world‑agnostic

be independently verifiable

Deprecation is a constitutional act.

2. Deprecation Must Preserve Identity

Deprecation cannot:

alter identityHash

rewrite canonical identity fields

create a new asset

collapse history

Identity persists even when the asset is no longer active.

3. Deprecation Must Preserve Provenance

Deprecation must:

append a deprecation event

preserve all prior history

maintain continuity

never remove or rewrite past events

Deprecation is the final chapter of provenance, not a replacement for it.

4. Deprecation Must Preserve Lineage

A deprecated asset must:

remain in the lineage graph

maintain all ancestors

maintain all descendants

maintain forks and merges

maintain world migrations

Lineage does not end when activity ends.

4.38.3 Deprecation States

An asset may enter one of several constitutional deprecation states:

1. Deprecated

The asset is no longer recommended for active use but remains functional.

2. Sunset

The asset is no longer functional but remains accessible for reference.

3. End‑of‑Life (EOL)

The asset is fully retired but remains canonical and verifiable.

4. Representational EOL

The asset’s content is no longer available, but its identity, provenance, and lineage remain intact.

Berry never erases truth — it represents finality without destruction.

4.38.4 Deprecation Triggers

Deprecation may occur when:

an asset is replaced by a newer version

a world retires a feature or system

compliance requires retirement

creators intentionally sunset content

multiverse transitions require canonical closure

resurrection is no longer possible

catastrophic corruption renders the asset inactive

Deprecation is a constitutional lifecycle event.

4.38.5 Deprecation Process

A valid deprecation must:

Verify identity

Log the deprecation event

Update integrityHash

Preserve provenance

Preserve lineage

Preserve custody state

Mark the asset’s deprecation status

Ensure world‑agnostic verifiability

Deprecation is a structured, constitutional transition.

4.38.6 Deprecation Violations

Any attempt to:

deprecate without logging

deprecate without updating integrity

deprecate silently

rewrite history during deprecation

collapse lineage

erase provenance

treat deprecation as deletion

…constitutes a deprecation violation.

Violations must:

be logged

trigger zero‑trust enforcement

escalate to Platform Governance

invalidate dependent claims

Deprecation violations are treated as attempts to erase or falsify truth.

4.38.7 Constitutional Principle

Deprecation is the end of activity, not the end of truth.If deprecation erases truth, the Constitution fails.
