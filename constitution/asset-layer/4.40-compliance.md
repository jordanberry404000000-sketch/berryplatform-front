4.40 Asset Compliance, Regulatory Mapping & Legal Alignment Policy

4.40.1 Purpose

The Asset Compliance, Regulatory Mapping & Legal Alignment Policy defines how the Berry Platform™ satisfies legal, regulatory, and jurisdictional requirements without compromising identity, provenance, lineage, integrity, or constitutional truth.

Compliance in Berry is representational, not destructive.Legal requirements may shape how truth is presented, but they may never alter truth itself.

This policy ensures that Berry can operate globally while remaining constitutionally uncompromised.

4.40.2 Why Compliance Must Be Constitutional

Traditional systems handle compliance by:

deleting data

overwriting history

mutating records

collapsing lineage

destroying evidence

breaking continuity

These approaches violate digital truth.

Berry introduces a new model:

Compliance is a representational transformation, not a destructive action.

4.40.3 Constitutional Requirements

1. Compliance Must Not Destroy Truth

Compliance actions may:

redact

mask

obfuscate

restrict access

transform representation

…but they may not:

delete canonical history

alter identity

rewrite provenance

collapse lineage

break integrity

fabricate replacement data

Truth is immutable even when visibility changes.

2. Compliance Must Be Logged

Every compliance action must:

be logged in provenance

update integrityHash

include actor identity

include jurisdictional basis

include legal justification

be cryptographically signed

Compliance is not silent.

3. Compliance Must Be Reversible

Reversibility does not mean restoring visibility — it means:

the system can reconstruct the pre‑compliance state

auditors can verify canonical truth

lineage remains intact

integrity remains verifiable

Compliance cannot destroy the ability to prove truth.

4. Compliance Must Be Jurisdiction‑Aware

Compliance transformations must:

specify the jurisdiction

specify the legal basis

specify the scope

specify the affected fields

specify the representational method

Berry does not guess — it documents.

4.40.4 Types of Compliance Transformations

1. Redaction

Information is hidden but not destroyed.

2. Masking

Information is partially obscured.

3. Obfuscation

Information is transformed into a non‑identifying representation.

4. Access Restriction

Information remains canonical but is not accessible to certain actors.

5. Representational Replacement

A placeholder replaces sensitive data while preserving canonical truth.

6. Jurisdictional Forking

Different jurisdictions may view different representations of the same canonical asset.

All transformations must preserve identity, provenance, lineage, and integrity.

4.40.5 Compliance Mapping

Berry must support mapping to:

GDPR

CCPA

CPRA

HIPAA

PCI‑DSS

SOC 2

ISO 27001

jurisdiction‑specific privacy laws

industry‑specific regulatory frameworks

Mapping defines how compliance transforms representation — not truth.

4.40.6 Compliance Violations

Any attempt to:

delete canonical history

rewrite identity

collapse lineage

alter provenance

bypass logging

fabricate replacement data

perform silent compliance actions

…constitutes a compliance violation.

Violations must:

be logged

trigger zero‑trust enforcement

escalate to Platform Governance

invalidate dependent claims

Compliance violations are treated as attempts to destroy truth.

4.40.7 Constitutional Principle

Compliance may change what is visible.Compliance may not change what is true.
