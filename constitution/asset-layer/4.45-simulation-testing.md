4.45 Asset Simulation, Stress Testing & Integrity Assurance Policy

4.45.1 Purpose

The Asset Simulation, Stress Testing & Integrity Assurance Policy defines how the Berry Platform™ validates the resilience, correctness, and survivability of assets under extreme conditions.

This policy ensures that Berry can withstand:

catastrophic failures

adversarial attacks

multiverse desynchronization

world‑level corruption

high‑volume mutation

resurrection conflicts

compliance transformations

cross‑system inconsistencies

Simulation is not optional — it is constitutional.

4.45.2 Why Simulation Exists

Truth is only meaningful if it survives pressure.

Berry must prove that:

identity cannot be forged

provenance cannot be rewritten

lineage cannot be collapsed

integrity cannot be bypassed

custody cannot be falsified

reconstruction cannot be abused

resurrection cannot be exploited

compliance cannot destroy truth

Simulation is the constitutional mechanism that validates these guarantees.

4.45.3 Constitutional Requirements

1. Mandatory Simulation Coverage

Every asset must be subject to:

identity verification simulations

provenance continuity simulations

lineage reconstruction simulations

integrity recomputation simulations

custody conflict simulations

mutation stress tests

resurrection edge‑case tests

compliance transformation tests

multiverse arbitration tests

Simulation is continuous, not periodic.

2. Simulation Must Be Deterministic

Given the same inputs, simulations must always produce the same results.

No nondeterministic behavior.No world‑specific outcomes.No probabilistic truth.

3. Simulation Must Be Forensic

Simulations must:

log all events

update integrityHash

validate signatures

detect tampering

detect missing history

detect lineage inconsistencies

detect custody conflicts

Simulations are constitutional evidence.

4.45.4 Stress Testing Requirements

Stress tests must evaluate:

1. High‑Volume Mutation

Rapid, repeated mutations must preserve:

identity

provenance

lineage

integrity

2. Fork/Merge Extremes

Complex branching must remain:

continuous

verifiable

reconstructable

3. Resurrection Edge Cases

Repeated resurrection attempts must:

preserve identity

preserve lineage

preserve provenance

update integrity correctly

4. Compliance Extremes

Heavy redaction must not:

destroy truth

break continuity

collapse lineage

5. Multiverse Desynchronization

Worlds falling out of sync must not:

produce contradictory truth

override canonical state

Stress tests prove survivability.

4.45.5 Integrity Assurance Requirements

Integrity assurance must:

recompute integrityHash

verify hash‑chained events

validate signatures

detect tampering

detect omissions

detect silent mutations

detect world‑level overrides

Integrity assurance is the mathematical defense of truth.

4.45.6 Simulation Violations

Any attempt to:

bypass simulation

falsify simulation results

suppress simulation logs

fabricate simulation evidence

override simulation outcomes

avoid stress testing

disable integrity assurance

…constitutes a simulation violation.

Violations must:

be logged

trigger zero‑trust enforcement

escalate to Platform Governance

invalidate dependent claims

Simulation violations are treated as attempts to hide fragility.

4.45.7 Constitutional Principle

Simulation proves truth.Stress testing protects truth.If truth cannot survive pressure, it was never truth at all.
