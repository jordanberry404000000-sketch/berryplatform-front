8.0 Dispute Resolution & Appeals Overview

8.0.1 Purpose

The Dispute Resolution & Appeals Layer defines how Berry Platform™ hears challenges, resolves conflicts, and corrects errors without rewriting history or compromising canonical truth.

This layer ensures that:

actors may challenge decisions

enforcement may be reviewed

governance may be appealed

evidence is evaluated fairly

outcomes are binding and constitutional

Dispute resolution is the institution’s commitment to fairness.

8.0.2 Philosophy

Berry’s dispute philosophy is built on three principles:

1. Truth Is Canonical, But Interpretation Is Reviewable

The platform never rewrites truth, but it may reinterpret evidence.

2. Enforcement Is Deterministic, But Not Infallible

Enforcement actions may be challenged when evidence or authority is disputed.

3. Governance Is Final, But Not Beyond Question

Governance rulings may be appealed through constitutional channels.

Dispute resolution is not a loophole — it is a safeguard.

8.0.3 Scope

Dispute resolution applies to:

enforcement actions

sanctions

authority decisions

governance rulings

custody and lineage outcomes

world‑level conflicts

actor‑level disagreements

If it affects an actor’s rights, authority, or truth, it may be disputed.

8.0.4 Dispute Types

Berry recognizes three classes of disputes:

1. Factual Disputes

Challenges to evidence, signatures, or chain state.

2. Procedural Disputes

Challenges to how enforcement or governance acted.

3. Constitutional Disputes

Challenges to the interpretation of constitutional law.

Each type follows a different review path.

8.0.5 Appeals Structure

Appeals follow a strict hierarchy:

Local Review → System Review → Governance Review

Appeals may escalate only when:

evidence is insufficient

authority was misapplied

constitutional interpretation is required

Appeals cannot be suppressed.

8.0.6 Evidence Requirements

Every dispute must include:

disputed action or ruling

evidence snapshot

actor identity

timestamps

world‑context

justification for dispute

Disputes without evidence are invalid.

8.0.7 Outcomes

Dispute resolution may result in:

confirmation of the original decision

modification of the decision

reversal of the decision

issuance of new sanctions

governance interpretation

constitutional clarification

Outcomes must be logged and immutable.

8.0.8 Constitutional Principle

Fairness protects legitimacy.Legitimacy protects authority.Authority protects truth.
