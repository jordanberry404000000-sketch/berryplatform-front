8.1 Dispute Initiation

8.1.1 Purpose

Dispute Initiation defines how actors formally raise challenges within Berry Platform™.This chapter ensures that:

disputes begin in a structured, constitutional manner

actors have a clear path to challenge actions

evidence is captured at the moment of dispute

disputes cannot be ignored, suppressed, or mishandled

A dispute begins the moment an actor asserts:“This action, ruling, or outcome is incorrect.”

8.1.2 Who May Initiate a Dispute

Any actor may initiate a dispute if:

they are directly affected by an enforcement action

they are indirectly affected by a governance ruling

they believe evidence was misinterpreted

they believe authority was misapplied

they believe a constitutional principle was violated

Dispute rights are universal and cannot be revoked.

8.1.3 Dispute Preconditions

A dispute may be initiated only when:

an enforcement action has occurred

a governance ruling has been issued

a custody or lineage outcome has been finalized

a world‑level decision has impacted the actor

evidence exists to support the challenge

Disputes cannot be speculative or hypothetical.

8.1.4 Dispute Submission Requirements

A valid dispute submission must include:

Disputed Action ID

Actor Identity

Evidence Snapshot

Dispute Type (factual, procedural, constitutional)

Justification

Timestamps

World‑Context

Requested Review Level

Submissions missing any required field are rejected automatically.

8.1.5 Dispute Types at Initiation

Actors must classify their dispute as one of:

1. Factual Dispute

“Evidence, signatures, or chain state are incorrect.”

2. Procedural Dispute

“Enforcement or governance acted incorrectly.”

3. Constitutional Dispute

“The ruling contradicts constitutional law.”

Classification determines the review path.

8.1.6 Immediate Effects of Dispute Initiation

Upon initiation:

the dispute is logged immutably

the disputed action is flagged for review

enforcement is notified

system or governance may be alerted depending on severity

evidence is frozen to prevent tampering

Disputes do not pause enforcement unless explicitly allowed by constitutional rules.

8.1.7 Invalid Disputes

A dispute is invalid if:

evidence is missing

the actor is not affected

the dispute is malicious or repetitive

the dispute contradicts immutable truth

the dispute attempts to rewrite history

Invalid disputes are rejected with justification.

8.1.8 Constitutional Principle

The right to challenge is the foundation of fairness.The right to be heard is the foundation of legitimacy.
