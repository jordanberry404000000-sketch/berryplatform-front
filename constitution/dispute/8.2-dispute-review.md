8.2 Dispute Review

8.2.1 Purpose

Dispute Review defines how Berry evaluates disputes at each authority level.This chapter ensures that:

disputes are reviewed fairly

evidence is evaluated objectively

authority is applied correctly

outcomes are consistent and constitutional

no dispute is ignored or mishandled

Review is the institution’s commitment to due process.

8.2.2 Review Levels

Disputes follow a strict three‑tier review model:

1. Local Review

Handles factual and procedural disputes with limited scope.

2. System Review

Handles disputes involving cross‑world impact, authority misuse, or structural inconsistencies.

3. Governance Review

Handles constitutional disputes, high‑severity appeals, and rulings that affect institutional integrity.

Each level has distinct powers and responsibilities.

8.2.3 Review Triggers

A dispute enters review when:

a valid dispute submission is received

evidence is complete

the dispute type is classified

the appropriate review level is determined

Review cannot be delayed or suppressed.

8.2.4 Review Requirements

Every review must include:

the full dispute submission

the disputed action or ruling

all associated evidence

authority snapshots

chain state

world‑context

timestamps

reviewer identity

review justification

Reviews must be reproducible and auditable.

8.2.5 Review Standards

Berry applies three standards depending on dispute type:

1. Evidence Standard (Factual Disputes)

“Does the evidence support the disputed action?”

2. Procedure Standard (Procedural Disputes)

“Was the action performed correctly according to constitutional rules?”

3. Constitutional Standard (Constitutional Disputes)

“Does the ruling align with constitutional principles?”

Standards must be applied consistently.

8.2.6 Review Outcomes

A review may result in:

Confirmation — the original decision stands

Modification — the decision is adjusted

Reversal — the decision is overturned

Escalation — the dispute moves to a higher review level

Clarification — constitutional interpretation is issued

All outcomes must be logged immutably.

8.2.7 Review Failure Modes

Berry must detect and prevent:

biased reviews

incomplete reviews

forged review outcomes

review suppression

review tampering

selective review

circular review loops

Any attempt to interfere with review is a constitutional violation.

8.2.8 Constitutional Principle

Review is not a courtesy.Review is a right.Review is how institutions remain just.
