8.3 Dispute Resolution

8.3.1 Purpose

Dispute Resolution defines how Berry issues final decisions on disputes after review.This chapter ensures that:

outcomes are fair

outcomes are consistent

outcomes are constitutional

outcomes are logged immutably

outcomes cannot be suppressed or altered

Resolution is the moment the institution speaks.

8.3.2 Resolution Authority

Resolution authority depends on the dispute level:

Local Resolution

Handles factual corrections and minor procedural issues.

System Resolution

Handles structural inconsistencies, authority misuse, and cross‑world disputes.

Governance Resolution

Handles constitutional disputes, high‑severity appeals, and rulings that affect institutional integrity.

Resolution authority must match dispute severity.

8.3.3 Resolution Requirements

Every resolution must include:

dispute identifier

review findings

evidence summary

constitutional references (if applicable)

final decision

justification

authority signatures

timestamps

world‑context

impact assessment

Resolutions must be complete, reproducible, and auditable.

8.3.4 Resolution Standards

Berry applies three standards depending on dispute type:

1. Factual Standard

“Does the evidence support the corrected outcome?”

2. Procedural Standard

“Was the correct constitutional process followed?”

3. Constitutional Standard

“Does the outcome align with constitutional principles?”

Standards must be applied consistently across all worlds and actors.

8.3.5 Resolution Outcomes

A dispute may be resolved with one of the following outcomes:

1. Confirmation

The original decision stands.

2. Modification

The decision is adjusted to reflect corrected evidence or procedure.

3. Reversal

The decision is overturned entirely.

4. Escalation to Governance

Used when constitutional interpretation is required.

5. Constitutional Clarification

Governance issues a binding interpretation that becomes part of constitutional precedent.

6. Sanction Adjustment

Sanctions may be increased, decreased, or removed based on findings.

Resolution outcomes must be final unless appealed through constitutional channels.

8.3.6 Resolution Logging

Every resolution must be:

logged immutably

hash‑linked

cryptographically signed

globally consistent

accessible to the appropriate transparency tier

If a resolution is not logged, it is invalid.

8.3.7 Resolution Failure Modes

Berry must detect and prevent:

forged resolutions

unauthorized resolutions

resolution suppression

resolution tampering

selective resolution

contradictory resolutions

resolution loops

Any attempt to interfere with resolution is a constitutional violation.

8.3.8 Constitutional Principle

Resolution is where fairness becomes truth.Truth becomes law.Law becomes institutional memory.
