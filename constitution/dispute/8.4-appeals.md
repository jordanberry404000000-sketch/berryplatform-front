8.4 Appeals

8.4.1 Purpose

The Appeals chapter defines how actors challenge dispute resolutions and seek higher‑level review.This ensures that:

no decision is beyond question

actors have a structured path to escalate

governance remains accountable

constitutional interpretation evolves

fairness is preserved across worlds

Appeals are the institution’s final safeguard against error.

8.4.2 Who May Appeal

An appeal may be filed by:

any actor directly affected by a resolution

any actor indirectly affected by a governance ruling

enforcement, when structural inconsistencies are detected

governance itself, when constitutional clarification is required

Appeal rights cannot be revoked.

8.4.3 Appeal Preconditions

An appeal may be submitted only when:

a dispute has been formally resolved

the actor provides new evidence or

the actor identifies procedural or constitutional error

the appeal is filed within constitutional time limits

the appeal is not malicious or repetitive

Appeals must be grounded in substance, not dissatisfaction.

8.4.4 Appeal Submission Requirements

A valid appeal must include:

Resolution ID

Actor Identity

Appeal Type (factual, procedural, constitutional)

New Evidence or Justification

Requested Review Level

Timestamps

World‑Context

Impact Statement

Appeals missing required fields are rejected automatically.

8.4.5 Appeal Types

Berry recognizes three appeal types:

1. Factual Appeal

“New evidence changes the interpretation of truth.”

2. Procedural Appeal

“The resolution process was incorrect or incomplete.”

3. Constitutional Appeal

“The resolution contradicts constitutional principles.”

Constitutional appeals always escalate to governance.

8.4.6 Appeal Review Levels

Appeals follow the same hierarchy as disputes:

Local Appeal Review

For minor factual or procedural corrections.

System Appeal Review

For structural inconsistencies or authority misuse.

Governance Appeal Review

For constitutional interpretation, high‑severity reversals, and precedent setting.

Appeals cannot be suppressed or rerouted.

8.4.7 Appeal Outcomes

An appeal may result in:

Denial — the resolution stands

Modification — the resolution is adjusted

Reversal — the resolution is overturned

Escalation — the appeal moves to a higher authority

Constitutional Interpretation — governance issues binding precedent

Institutional Correction — systemic or procedural rules are updated

Appeal outcomes must be final unless escalated constitutionally.

8.4.8 Appeal Logging

Every appeal must be:

logged immutably

hash‑linked

cryptographically signed

globally consistent

accessible to the appropriate transparency tier

If an appeal is not logged, it is invalid.

8.4.9 Appeal Failure Modes

Berry must detect and prevent:

forged appeals

unauthorized appeals

appeal suppression

appeal tampering

selective appeal handling

contradictory appeal outcomes

infinite appeal loops

Any interference with appeals is a constitutional violation.

8.4.10 Constitutional Principle

Appeals are the institution’s promise that no decision is final until fairness is satisfied.
