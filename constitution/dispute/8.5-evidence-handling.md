8.5 Evidence Handling

8.5.1 Purpose

Evidence Handling defines how Berry collects, preserves, evaluates, and protects evidence throughout disputes and appeals.This chapter ensures that:

evidence is reliable

evidence is tamper‑evident

evidence is preserved across worlds

evidence is evaluated consistently

evidence cannot be suppressed or altered

Evidence is the foundation of fairness.

8.5.2 Evidence Philosophy

Berry’s evidence model is built on three principles:

1. Evidence Must Outlive the Actor

Evidence remains valid even if actors, worlds, or processes are removed.

2. Evidence Must Be Immutable

Once captured, evidence cannot be altered, deleted, or rewritten.

3. Evidence Must Be Interpretable

Evidence must be structured so that any reviewer can understand it.

Evidence is not opinion — it is fact.

8.5.3 Evidence Types

Berry recognizes four classes of evidence:

1. Cryptographic Evidence

Signatures, hashes, chain state, lineage proofs.

2. Behavioral Evidence

Actions taken by actors, worlds, or systems.

3. Procedural Evidence

Logs, timestamps, authority snapshots, enforcement actions.

4. Contextual Evidence

World‑context, environmental state, dependency conditions.

All evidence must be captured at the moment of the event.

8.5.4 Evidence Capture Requirements

Every piece of evidence must include:

evidence identifier

actor identity

chain state

world‑context

timestamps

cryptographic signatures

capture method

integrity checks

If evidence is not captured correctly, it is invalid.

8.5.5 Evidence Preservation

Evidence must be:

immutable

hash‑linked

redundantly stored

globally consistent

accessible to authorized reviewers

preserved across system upgrades

Evidence cannot be deleted, overwritten, or hidden.

8.5.6 Evidence Evaluation Standards

Berry applies three evaluation standards:

1. Authenticity

“Is the evidence genuine and unaltered?”

2. Relevance

“Does the evidence directly relate to the dispute?”

3. Sufficiency

“Is the evidence adequate to support a decision?”

Evidence must meet all three standards.

8.5.7 Evidence Failure Modes

Berry must detect and prevent:

forged evidence

missing evidence

corrupted evidence

selectively withheld evidence

contradictory evidence

evidence tampering

evidence suppression

Any interference with evidence is a constitutional violation.

8.5.8 Evidence Access

Access to evidence follows the transparency model:

Local Access — actors may view evidence relevant to their own disputes

System Access — enforcement may view cross‑world evidence

Governance Access — governance may view all evidence without restriction

Evidence access must be logged.

8.5.9 Constitutional Principle

Evidence is the anchor of truth.Truth is the anchor of justice.Justice is the anchor of the institution.
