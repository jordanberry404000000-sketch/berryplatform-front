7.1 Enforcement Authority Model

7.1.1 Purpose

The Enforcement Authority Model defines who may execute enforcement actions, under what conditions, and with what constitutional legitimacy.Authority is not assumed.Authority is not implied.Authority must be proven, verifiable, and cryptographically anchored.

This chapter establishes the hierarchy of enforcement power within the Berry Platform™.

7.1.2 Authority Principles

Berry’s authority model is built on four constitutional principles:

1. Authority Must Be Explicit

No actor, process, or world may enforce anything without explicit, cryptographically verifiable authority.

2. Authority Must Be Minimal

Actors receive only the authority required for their role — nothing more.

3. Authority Must Be Non‑Transferable

Authority cannot be delegated, shared, or inherited without governance approval.

4. Authority Must Be Revocable

All authority can be suspended or revoked if misused or compromised.

Authority is a privilege, not a possession.

7.1.3 Enforcement Actors

Berry recognizes three classes of enforcement actors:

1. Local Enforcement Actors

These actors enforce rules within a single world or asset.

They may:

reject invalid actions

enforce local constraints

apply low‑severity sanctions

trigger escalation

They cannot:

modify authority

issue governance rulings

override canonical truth

Local enforcement is the first line of defense.

2. System Enforcement Actors

These actors enforce rules across the entire platform.

They may:

quarantine actors or worlds

suspend authority

enforce medium‑severity sanctions

initiate breach response

trigger governance escalation

They cannot:

issue final rulings

modify constitutional law

System enforcement is the backbone of institutional order.

3. Governance Enforcement Actors

These actors represent the highest enforcement authority.

They may:

issue binding rulings

revoke authority

impose high‑severity sanctions

authorize recovery actions

override system‑level decisions

modify enforcement policy

Governance enforcement is the constitutional apex.

7.1.4 Authority Verification

All enforcement authority must be:

cryptographically signed

bound to a specific actor

bound to a specific role

bound to a specific scope

validated before every enforcement action

Authority cannot be cached, assumed, or inferred.

7.1.5 Authority Escalation

Authority escalation must follow this path:

Local → System → Governance

Escalation is required when:

authority is insufficient

violations exceed local scope

actions impact canonical truth

sanctions exceed local limits

governance review is mandatory

Escalation is not optional — it is constitutional.

7.1.6 Authority Abuse

Any attempt to:

enforce without authority

exceed authority scope

forge authority

suppress enforcement

impersonate enforcement actors

…constitutes authority abuse.

Authority abuse is a constitutional violation and triggers:

immediate suspension

quarantine

governance escalation

potential revocation

Authority must be used correctly or not at all.

7.1.7 Constitutional Principle

Authority must be legitimate.Legitimacy must be verifiable.Verification must be absolute.
