7.2 Enforcement Actions

7.2.1 Purpose

Enforcement Actions define the mechanisms Berry Platform™ uses to uphold constitutional law, respond to violations, and maintain institutional order.These actions are the operational tools of enforcement — the levers of authority that ensure rules are not merely written, but executed.

Enforcement Actions must be:

deterministic

cryptographically verifiable

bound to legitimate authority

logged and tamper‑evident

consistent across all worlds and actors

Enforcement is not discretionary.It is constitutional.

7.2.2 Enforcement Action Categories

Berry recognizes six classes of enforcement actions:

1. Rejection Actions

These actions prevent invalid or unauthorized operations from executing.

Examples:

reject invalid signatures

reject broken chains

reject unauthorized mutations

reject actions outside authority scope

Rejection is the most common enforcement action — the first line of defense.

2. Quarantine Actions

These actions isolate actors, assets, or worlds to prevent further harm.

Quarantine may apply to:

compromised actors

corrupted assets

hostile worlds

suspicious processes

Quarantine is containment, not punishment.

3. Suspension Actions

These actions temporarily remove authority or functionality.

Suspension may apply to:

actor authority

world permissions

system processes

custody operations

Suspension is used when risk is present but not yet adjudicated.

4. Revocation Actions

These actions permanently remove authority or access.

Revocation may apply to:

actor roles

world privileges

enforcement permissions

governance keys (with governance approval)

Revocation is the strongest non‑sanction enforcement action.

5. Sanction Actions

These actions impose consequences for violations.

Sanctions may include:

authority reduction

asset freezing

world restrictions

operational penalties

governance‑mandated outcomes

Sanctions are constitutional consequences, not discretionary punishment.

6. Escalation Actions

These actions elevate enforcement to higher authority levels.

Escalation occurs when:

violations exceed local scope

canonical truth is threatened

authority is insufficient

governance review is required

Escalation is mandatory when conditions are met.

7.2.3 Enforcement Action Requirements

All enforcement actions must:

be cryptographically signed

include actor identity

include authority verification

include violation context

be logged immutably

be reproducible from evidence

No enforcement action may occur without:

legitimate authority

constitutional justification

verifiable evidence

Enforcement must be provable.

7.2.4 Enforcement Action Validity

An enforcement action is valid only if:

the actor has authority

the authority matches the action

the action matches the violation

the action is within constitutional limits

the action is logged

the action is verifiable

Invalid enforcement actions are themselves violations.

7.2.5 Enforcement Action Failure Modes

Berry must detect and prevent:

forged enforcement actions

unauthorized enforcement

selective enforcement

enforcement suppression

enforcement tampering

inconsistent enforcement outcomes

Any attempt to manipulate enforcement actions is treated as a constitutional breach.

7.2.6 Constitutional Principle

Rules mean nothing without enforcement.Enforcement means nothing without authority.Authority means nothing without truth.
