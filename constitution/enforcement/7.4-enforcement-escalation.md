7.4 Enforcement Escalation

7.4.1 Purpose

Enforcement Escalation defines how, when, and why enforcement actions move from one authority level to the next.Escalation ensures that:

violations are handled by the correct authority

threats are not underestimated

constitutional risks are not ignored

governance receives what governance must adjudicate

Escalation is the backbone of institutional order.

7.4.2 Escalation Philosophy

Berry’s escalation model is built on three principles:

1. Authority Must Match Severity

Low‑severity violations stay local.High‑severity violations rise automatically.

2. Escalation Must Be Mandatory

If conditions are met, escalation must occur — no actor may suppress or delay it.

3. Escalation Must Be Evidence‑Driven

Escalation is triggered by facts, not opinions.

7.4.3 Escalation Levels

Berry recognizes three escalation levels:

Level 1 → Level 2: Local to System Escalation

Triggered when:

violations impact multiple worlds

authority is insufficient

chain inconsistencies appear

custody or lineage is compromised

local enforcement is bypassed or suppressed

System enforcement takes over when the violation exceeds local boundaries.

Level 2 → Level 3: System to Governance Escalation

Triggered when:

canonical truth is threatened

governance authority is challenged

enforcement suppression is detected

authority impersonation occurs

system‑level enforcement is insufficient

Governance escalation is the highest form of institutional review.

Direct Escalation (Exceptional Cases)

Some violations bypass lower levels entirely.

Examples:

forged governance actions

tampering with canonical truth

attempts to rewrite chains

attempts to disable enforcement

These are constitutional violations and go straight to governance.

7.4.4 Escalation Triggers

Escalation is triggered by:

violation severity

violation scope

authority mismatch

evidence thresholds

system alerts

governance mandates

Triggers must be deterministic and reproducible.

7.4.5 Escalation Evidence Requirements

Every escalation must include:

violation payload

actor identity

authority snapshot

world‑context

chain state

timestamps

enforcement history

justification for escalation

Escalation without evidence is invalid.

7.4.6 Escalation Failure Modes

Berry must detect and prevent:

escalation suppression

selective escalation

forged escalation requests

escalation loops

escalation deadlocks

escalation tampering

Any attempt to interfere with escalation is a constitutional violation.

7.4.7 Escalation Outcomes

Escalation may result in:

system‑level enforcement

governance rulings

sanctions

revocation

recovery actions

constitutional review

Escalation always leads to action — never silence.

7.4.8 Constitutional Principle

Local handles the small.System handles the structural.Governance handles the existential.
