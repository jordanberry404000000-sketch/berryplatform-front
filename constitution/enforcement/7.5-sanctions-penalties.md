7.5 Sanctions & Penalties

7.5.1 Purpose

Sanctions & Penalties define the consequences applied when actors violate constitutional rules within the Berry Platform™.Sanctions are not emotional, punitive, or discretionary.They are institutional responses designed to:

protect canonical truth

preserve system integrity

uphold governance authority

deter adversarial behavior

maintain order across worlds

Sanctions are the constitutional teeth of enforcement.

7.5.2 Sanction Philosophy

Berry’s sanction model is built on three principles:

1. Sanctions Must Be Proportionate

The severity of the sanction must match the severity of the violation.

2. Sanctions Must Be Deterministic

Given the same violation, the same sanction must always apply.

3. Sanctions Must Be Non‑Repudiable

Sanctions must be backed by evidence, signatures, and immutable logs.

Sanctions are not opinions — they are outcomes.

7.5.3 Sanction Categories

Berry recognizes five classes of sanctions:

1. Authority Reduction

Reduces the actor’s authority scope.

Used for:

repeated minor violations

misuse of local authority

boundary‑testing behavior

Authority reduction is corrective, not punitive.

2. Operational Restrictions

Limits what an actor or world may do.

Examples:

restricted custody operations

limited mutation permissions

reduced world privileges

Operational restrictions prevent further harm.

3. Asset Freezing

Temporarily or permanently freezes assets or worlds.

Used for:

custody violations

lineage corruption

adversarial asset behavior

Freezing protects canonical truth.

4. Role Suspension or Revocation

Temporarily or permanently removes actor roles.

Used for:

authority abuse

impersonation attempts

enforcement suppression

Role revocation is a high‑severity sanction.

5. Governance‑Mandated Outcomes

The highest class of sanctions.

Examples:

permanent actor removal

world deactivation

irreversible authority revocation

constitutional rulings

These sanctions reshape the institution.

7.5.4 Sanction Requirements

Every sanction must include:

violation identifier

actor identity

authority snapshot

evidence payload

sanction type

justification

timestamps

world‑context

enforcement signatures

Sanctions must be fully auditable.

7.5.5 Sanction Escalation

Sanctions escalate when:

violations repeat

violations increase in severity

violations impact multiple worlds

violations threaten canonical truth

authority is abused

enforcement is suppressed

Escalation is mandatory when thresholds are met.

7.5.6 Sanction Failure Modes

Berry must detect and prevent:

forged sanctions

unauthorized sanctions

selective sanctions

sanction suppression

sanction tampering

sanction reversal without governance approval

Any attempt to manipulate sanctions is a constitutional violation.

7.5.7 Constitutional Principle

Sanctions protect order.Order protects truth.Truth protects the institution.
