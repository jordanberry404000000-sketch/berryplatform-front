7.6 Enforcement Logging & Transparency

7.6.1 Purpose

Enforcement Logging & Transparency defines how Berry Platform™ records, exposes, and preserves every enforcement action, violation, sanction, and escalation.This layer ensures that:

enforcement is visible

enforcement is auditable

enforcement is tamper‑evident

enforcement cannot be suppressed

enforcement cannot be rewritten

Transparency is not optional — it is constitutional.

7.6.2 Logging Philosophy

Berry’s logging model is built on three principles:

1. Nothing Happens in the Dark

Every enforcement action must be logged, no exceptions.

2. Logs Must Outlive Actors

Logs must remain intact even if actors, worlds, or processes are removed.

3. Transparency Protects Legitimacy

Enforcement without transparency is indistinguishable from abuse.

7.6.3 Logging Requirements

Every enforcement log must include:

enforcement action type

actor identity

authority snapshot

violation identifier

evidence payload

world‑context

chain state

timestamps

escalation history

enforcement signatures

Logs must be:

immutable

canonical

cryptographically anchored

globally consistent

reproducible

If it isn’t logged, it didn’t happen.

7.6.4 Log Types

Berry maintains four classes of enforcement logs:

1. Action Logs

Record every enforcement action taken.

Examples:

rejection

quarantine

suspension

revocation

sanction

escalation

2. Violation Logs

Record every violation detected.

Include:

violation type

rule identifier

evidence snapshot

severity classification

3. Authority Logs

Record all authority changes.

Include:

role assignments

authority revocations

authority escalations

governance approvals

Authority logs are the backbone of institutional legitimacy.

4. Governance Logs

Record all governance‑level enforcement decisions.

Include:

rulings

sanctions

constitutional interpretations

recovery authorizations

Governance logs are the highest form of institutional record.

7.6.5 Log Integrity

Logs must be:

tamper‑evident

cryptographically signed

hash‑linked

immutable

globally verifiable

Any attempt to modify, delete, or reorder logs is a constitutional violation.

7.6.6 Log Access & Transparency

Berry enforces a tiered transparency model:

Local Transparency

Actors may view logs relevant to their own actions.

System Transparency

System enforcement may view cross‑world logs.

Governance Transparency

Governance may view all logs without restriction.

Public Transparency (Selective)

Certain logs may be exposed publicly to demonstrate institutional integrity.

Transparency is a constitutional right — and a constitutional responsibility.

7.6.7 Logging Failure Modes

Berry must detect and prevent:

log deletion

log suppression

log tampering

log forgery

log reordering

log withholding

log corruption

Any attempt to interfere with logs is treated as hostile behavior.

7.6.8 Constitutional Principle

Enforcement without logs is tyranny.Logs without transparency are meaningless.Transparency without integrity is deception.
