5.0 Governance Overview

5.0.1 Purpose

The Governance Layer establishes the constitutional framework through which authority, decision‑making, enforcement, and institutional oversight are exercised within the Berry Platform™.It defines who may act, how they may act, and the limits placed upon all actors, ensuring that no individual, system, world, or process may override constitutional truth.

This layer governs the exercise of power, not the definition of assets.It ensures that governance actions remain transparent, verifiable, and subordinate to the Constitution.

5.0.2 Scope

This Governance Layer applies to:

all actors interacting with the platform

all worlds and world‑level systems

all custodial entities

all governance bodies

all automated or autonomous processes

all integrations and external systems

all constitutional subsystems

Governance authority is universal and cannot be bypassed, delegated, or overridden by external policy.

5.0.3 Governance Principles

1. Constitutional Supremacy

All governance actions must comply with the Constitution.No actor or system may override constitutional truth.

2. Zero‑Trust Authority

Governance assumes no inherent trust in any actor.Authority must be verified, logged, and cryptographically anchored.

3. Deterministic Enforcement

Governance decisions must be deterministic, reproducible, and evidence‑based.

4. Transparency & Accountability

All governance actions must be logged, signed, and independently verifiable.

5. Separation of Powers

Authority is distributed across roles and systems to prevent unilateral control.

6. Non‑Destructive Governance

Governance actions may restrict, redact, or transform representation, but may not destroy canonical truth.

5.0.4 Governance Functions

The Governance Layer provides the constitutional framework for:

defining actor roles and permissions

authorizing and restricting actions

resolving disputes and conflicts

enforcing compliance and security

escalating violations

maintaining institutional continuity

ensuring constitutional integrity

Governance is the mechanism through which the platform protects itself from misuse, corruption, or unauthorized authority.

5.0.5 Governance Boundaries

Governance authority is limited by:

constitutional requirements

cryptographic verification

provenance and lineage constraints

integrity enforcement

zero‑trust assumptions

Governance may not:

alter identity

rewrite provenance

collapse lineage

bypass integrity

fabricate authority

suppress constitutional logs

Governance is powerful, but not absolute.

5.0.6 Governance Violations

Any attempt to:

exercise unauthorized authority

bypass governance processes

falsify governance records

suppress governance logs

override constitutional constraints

fabricate permissions

manipulate governance outcomes

…constitutes a governance violation.

Violations must be:

logged

escalated

adjudicated

enforced deterministically

Governance violations are treated as existential threats to institutional integrity.

5.0.7 Constitutional Principle

Governance exists to constrain power, not to expand it.Authority must always serve truth — never the reverse.
