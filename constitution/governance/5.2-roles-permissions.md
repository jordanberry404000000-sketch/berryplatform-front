5.2 Actor Roles & Permissions

5.2.1 Purpose

The Actor Roles & Permissions Policy defines the constitutionally recognized actors within the Berry Platform™ and establishes the precise permissions, limitations, and responsibilities assigned to each role.This policy ensures that all actions are performed by authorized actors operating within clearly defined boundaries, consistent with the Authority Model (5.1).

Roles are constitutional constructs.Permissions are explicit, not implied.No actor may exceed the scope of their role.

5.2.2 Recognized Actor Roles

Berry recognizes six primary actor roles:

Creator

Custodian

World

System Process

Auditor

Governance Body

Each role carries distinct permissions and constitutional constraints.

5.2.3 Role Definitions & Permissions

1. Creator

Creators originate assets and define their initial canonical truth.

Permissions:

create new assets

define initial identity fields

establish initial metadata

initiate provenance

initiate lineage

Restrictions:

may not alter identity after creation

may not rewrite provenance

may not collapse lineage

may not bypass integrity

may not exercise governance authority

Creators initiate truth but do not control it.

2. Custodian

Custodians hold, manage, or transfer assets on behalf of creators or other actors.

Permissions:

hold custody

transfer custody

initiate mutations (within constitutional limits)

manage representational visibility

Restrictions:

may not alter identity

may not rewrite provenance

may not collapse lineage

may not fabricate custody events

may not perform governance actions

Custodians manage assets but do not define truth.

3. World

A world is an environment or ecosystem in which assets operate, evolve, or interact.

Permissions:

apply world‑specific metadata

execute world‑level processes

initiate world‑context events

enforce world‑level policies (within constitutional limits)

Restrictions:

may not override canonical truth

may not alter identity, provenance, lineage, or integrity

may not suppress constitutional logs

may not fabricate world‑context history

Worlds provide context, not authority.

4. System Process

Automated or autonomous processes operating under constitutional rules.

Permissions:

execute deterministic actions

validate signatures

recompute integrityHash

enforce constitutional constraints

perform automated reconciliation

Restrictions:

may not act without deterministic rules

may not exercise discretionary authority

may not fabricate events

may not bypass governance oversight

System processes enforce truth but do not interpret it.

5. Auditor

Auditors verify compliance, integrity, and constitutional correctness.

Permissions:

inspect canonical truth

verify provenance and lineage

validate integrity

review custody chains

access governance logs

Restrictions:

may not modify assets

may not alter logs

may not exercise governance authority

may not initiate mutations

Auditors observe truth but do not change it.

6. Governance Body

The entity responsible for oversight, enforcement, adjudication, and constitutional interpretation.

Permissions:

adjudicate disputes

enforce violations

revoke authority

authorize exceptional actions (within constitutional limits)

interpret constitutional rules

Restrictions:

may not alter canonical truth

may not fabricate authority

may not suppress logs

may not override cryptographic verification

may not exercise creative or custodial functions

Governance protects truth but does not create it.

5.2.4 Role Assignment

Role assignment must be:

explicit

logged

signed

verifiable

revocable

No actor may assume a role without constitutional authorization.

5.2.5 Role Separation

Actors may not combine roles unless explicitly permitted by governance statute.This prevents:

concentration of power

conflicts of interest

unilateral authority

governance capture

Separation of roles is a constitutional safeguard.

5.2.6 Role Violations

Any attempt to:

perform actions outside one’s role

impersonate another role

fabricate permissions

bypass role verification

suppress role‑related logs

combine roles without authorization

…constitutes a role violation.

Violations must trigger:

logging

enforcement

escalation

adjudication

Role violations are treated as attempts to subvert institutional order.

5.2.7 Constitutional Principle

Roles define authority.Permissions define limits.No actor may exceed the role the Constitution grants.
