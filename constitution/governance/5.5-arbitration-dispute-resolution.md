5.5 Governance Arbitration & Dispute Resolution

5.5.1 Purpose

The Governance Arbitration & Dispute Resolution Policy establishes the constitutional framework for resolving conflicts, disputes, and governance‑level disagreements within the Berry Platform™.It ensures that all disputes are adjudicated fairly, deterministically, and in accordance with constitutional principles.

Arbitration protects institutional order.Dispute resolution protects constitutional continuity.

5.5.2 Scope

This policy applies to disputes involving:

governance bodies

custodians

creators

auditors

worlds

system processes

authority assignments

enforcement outcomes

constitutional interpretations

If a conflict affects governance, it falls under this policy.

5.5.3 Arbitration Principles

1. Constitutional Primacy

All arbitration decisions must be grounded in constitutional rules.No arbitration outcome may override or reinterpret canonical truth.

2. Deterministic Resolution

Arbitration outcomes must be reproducible, evidence‑based, and cryptographically verifiable.

3. Neutral Governance

Arbitration must be conducted by governance actors with no conflict of interest.

4. Transparency

All arbitration actions must be logged as governance events.

5. Non‑Destructive Outcomes

Arbitration may restrict authority or permissions but may not alter canonical truth.

5.5.4 Arbitration Triggers

Arbitration must be initiated when:

two or more actors dispute authority

enforcement outcomes are contested

governance decisions conflict

constitutional interpretation is unclear

world‑level policies conflict with constitutional rules

system processes produce inconsistent outcomes

custody or lineage disputes arise

Arbitration is mandatory when disputes affect governance integrity.

5.5.5 Arbitration Process

Arbitration proceeds through the following constitutional stages:

1. Dispute Submission

The dispute must be submitted as a governance event containing:

actors involved

disputed action

constitutional basis

evidence

requested remedy

2. Evidence Review

Governance must review:

provenance

lineage

integrityHash

custody chain

enforcement logs

role assignments

authority grants

Evidence must be complete and cryptographically verifiable.

3. Constitutional Analysis

Governance must determine:

which constitutional rules apply

whether authority was exceeded

whether enforcement was correct

whether roles were violated

whether logs are consistent

4. Deterministic Ruling

The ruling must:

cite constitutional rules

specify violations (if any)

define corrective actions

define authority changes

define enforcement requirements

5. Enforcement of Outcome

The ruling must be:

logged

signed

enforced

propagated to all relevant systems

Arbitration outcomes are binding.

5.5.6 Dispute Resolution Outcomes

Possible outcomes include:

authority clarification

authority revocation

enforcement confirmation

enforcement reversal

custodial reassignment

world‑level restriction

system quarantine

constitutional interpretation ruling

Outcomes must be non‑destructive and constitutionally grounded.

5.5.7 Arbitration Violations

Any attempt to:

bypass arbitration

influence arbitration outcomes

suppress arbitration logs

fabricate evidence

interfere with governance review

override arbitration rulings

…constitutes an arbitration violation.

Violations must trigger:

zero‑trust enforcement

governance escalation

invalidation of dependent actions

Arbitration violations are treated as attempts to undermine institutional justice.

5.5.8 Constitutional Principle

Arbitration protects order.Dispute resolution protects fairness.Governance must resolve conflict without altering truth.
