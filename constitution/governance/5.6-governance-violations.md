5.6 Governance Violations

5.6.1 Purpose

The Governance Violations Policy defines the actions, omissions, and behaviors that constitute breaches of governance authority within the Berry Platform™.These violations represent the most severe threats to institutional integrity, constitutional order, and the platform’s zero‑trust governance model.

Governance violations are not operational errors.They are constitutional offenses.

5.6.2 Definition of a Governance Violation

A governance violation occurs when any actor, system, or world attempts to:

exceed governance authority

undermine governance processes

interfere with enforcement

manipulate governance outcomes

suppress governance evidence

fabricate authority or permissions

override constitutional constraints

If an action threatens institutional order, it is a governance violation.

5.6.3 Categories of Governance Violations

Berry recognizes five primary categories of governance violations:

1. Authority Violations

Actions that exceed or fabricate governance authority, including:

unauthorized role assignment

unauthorized authority grant

unauthorized authority revocation

impersonation of governance actors

bypassing authority verification

Authority violations are treated as attempts to seize unconstitutional power.

2. Enforcement Violations

Actions that interfere with constitutional enforcement, including:

disabling enforcement mechanisms

bypassing enforcement checks

suppressing enforcement logs

overriding enforcement outcomes

fabricating enforcement evidence

Enforcement violations undermine the Constitution’s defense mechanisms.

3. Logging Violations

Actions that compromise governance logs, including:

altering governance logs

deleting governance logs

fabricating governance logs

breaking hash‑chain continuity

creating unlogged governance actions

Logging violations are treated as attempts to erase institutional memory.

4. Adjudication Violations

Actions that interfere with dispute resolution, including:

influencing arbitration outcomes

suppressing evidence

bypassing adjudication

overriding governance rulings

fabricating constitutional interpretations

Adjudication violations threaten institutional justice.

5. Constitutional Violations

Actions that directly contradict constitutional rules, including:

overriding canonical truth

altering identity, provenance, lineage, or integrity

suppressing constitutional evidence

fabricating canonical history

bypassing zero‑trust assumptions

Constitutional violations are the highest‑severity governance offenses.

5.6.4 Detection Requirements

Governance violations must be detected through:

pre‑action verification

real‑time enforcement

post‑action validation

governance event analysis

integrityHash recomputation

hash‑chain continuity checks

auditor review

Detection must be deterministic and evidence‑based.

5.6.5 Enforcement Requirements

When a governance violation is detected:

Immediate RejectionThe unconstitutional action must be blocked.

Mandatory LoggingEvidence must be captured and recorded as a governance event.

Zero‑Trust EnforcementThe actor’s authority must be restricted pending review.

Governance EscalationThe violation must be escalated to the Governance Body.

AdjudicationGovernance must issue a binding ruling.

Institutional RemediationDependent actions must be invalidated or corrected.

Enforcement is mandatory and cannot be suppressed.

5.6.6 Severity Levels

Governance violations are classified into three severity levels:

Level 1 — Procedural Violations

Minor breaches of governance process without impact on truth or authority.

Level 2 — Structural Violations

Breaches that affect authority, enforcement, or governance continuity.

Level 3 — Constitutional Violations

Breaches that threaten canonical truth, institutional integrity, or constitutional supremacy.

Level 3 violations represent existential threats to the platform.

5.6.7 Governance Violation Outcomes

Possible outcomes include:

authority restriction

authority revocation

custodial reassignment

system quarantine

world‑level restriction

enforcement reinforcement

constitutional remediation

permanent governance sanctions

Outcomes must be non‑destructive to canonical truth.

5.6.8 Constitutional Principle

Governance violations are attacks on institutional order.The Constitution must respond with clarity, evidence, and uncompromising enforcement.
