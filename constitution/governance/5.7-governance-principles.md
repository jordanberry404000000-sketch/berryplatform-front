5.7 Governance Principles

5.7.1 Purpose

The Governance Principles establish the constitutional philosophy that guides all governance actions within the Berry Platform™.These principles define the intent, boundaries, and ethical foundations of governance authority, ensuring that all decisions serve truth, transparency, and institutional integrity.

Governance is not merely procedural.Governance is principled.

5.7.2 Principle of Constitutional Supremacy

The Constitution is the highest authority within the platform.All governance actions must comply with constitutional rules, and no actor — including governance bodies — may override or reinterpret canonical truth.

Truth governs authority, not the reverse.

5.7.3 Principle of Zero‑Trust Authority

Governance assumes no inherent trust in any actor, system, or world.Authority must be:

granted

verified

logged

constrained

Zero‑trust is not a security model.Zero‑trust is a constitutional doctrine.

5.7.4 Principle of Least‑Privilege Governance

Governance must exercise only the authority necessary to enforce constitutional rules.Excess authority is unconstitutional, even when exercised with good intent.

Power must be limited by design.

5.7.5 Principle of Deterministic Governance

Governance decisions must be:

reproducible

evidence‑based

cryptographically anchored

free from discretion or ambiguity

Governance must operate as a system of rules, not a system of opinions.

5.7.6 Principle of Transparency & Accountability

All governance actions must be:

logged

signed

auditable

independently verifiable

Governance without transparency is illegitimate.Governance without accountability is unconstitutional.

5.7.7 Principle of Non‑Destructive Governance

Governance may:

restrict authority

revoke permissions

enforce violations

adjudicate disputes

Governance may not:

alter identity

rewrite provenance

collapse lineage

fabricate history

suppress constitutional evidence

Governance protects truth; it does not modify it.

5.7.8 Principle of Institutional Continuity

Governance must ensure that:

constitutional rules persist

institutional memory is preserved

governance logs remain intact

authority transitions are orderly

enforcement remains consistent

Continuity is a constitutional requirement, not an operational preference.

5.7.9 Principle of Fair & Neutral Adjudication

Governance must resolve disputes:

impartially

deterministically

without conflict of interest

based solely on constitutional evidence

Justice is not interpretive.Justice is constitutional.

5.7.10 Constitutional Principle

Governance exists to protect truth, constrain power, and preserve institutional order.Without principles, governance becomes authority.With principles, governance becomes law.
