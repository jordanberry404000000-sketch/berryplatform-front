The Berry Governance Spine

Purpose of the Governance Spine

The Governance Spine defines how authority, responsibility, and enforcement flow through the Berry Platform™. It is the backbone that connects worlds, assets, and multiverse operations to a single, coherent constitutional model.

It answers three questions:

Who decides?

What can they change?

How is that power constrained?

Governance Layers

Berry governance is structured into three primary layers:

World Governance

Platform Governance

Multiverse Governance

Each layer has sovereignty within its scope, but none can violate constitutional truth.

1. World Governance

Worlds are sovereign, but not absolute.

World operators define local rules, policies, and experiences.

They can set access controls, economic models, content rules, and interaction patterns.

They cannot alter canonical identity, provenance, lineage, or integrity of assets.

They cannot erase constitutional history — only request redactions that are representational, not destructive.

Worlds are free to govern themselves, but they are bound to Berry’s canonical truth.

2. Platform Governance

Platform governance is the constitutional authority of Berry itself.

Defines and maintains the Constitution.

Approves amendments and structural changes.

Enforces zero‑trust security and operational standards.

Oversees compliance representation and legal mapping.

Arbitrates disputes that exceed a single world’s scope.

Platform governance is not a feature — it is the institutional layer that ensures Berry behaves like an accountable system, not an unbounded protocol.

3. Multiverse Governance

Multiverse governance operates across worlds, timelines, and federated systems.

Resolves cross‑world conflicts and canonical disputes.

Maintains multiverse‑wide coherence of identity, provenance, and lineage.

Defines how assets, worlds, and systems interact across boundaries.

Ensures no single world can unilaterally rewrite shared truth.

Multiverse governance is where Berry becomes more than a platform — it becomes an arbiter of digital reality across entire ecosystems.

Enforcement Hierarchy

When conflicts arise, enforcement follows this order:

World‑level enforcement — local rules, local remedies.

Platform‑level enforcement — constitutional alignment, security, compliance.

Multiverse‑level enforcement — canonical arbitration, cross‑world resolution.

At every level, enforcement is:

logged

auditable

reconstructable

bound by the Constitution

No enforcement action may violate canonical truth.

Appeals and Oversight

World decisions may be appealed to Platform Governance.

Platform decisions may be escalated to Multiverse Governance where cross‑world impact exists.

All decisions must be explainable, reconstructable, and anchored in constitutional policy.

There is no “silent power” in Berry.Every authority is named, scoped, and constrained.

Zero‑Trust Governance

Berry assumes:

no actor is inherently trusted

no system is inherently safe

no world is inherently aligned

Trust is earned through:

verifiable logs

cryptographic guarantees

constitutional compliance

reconstructable history

Governance in Berry is not about belief — it is about provable behavior.
