6.8.2 Resilience Doctrine

Berry’s resilience model is built on four principles:

1. Truth Must Survive

Canonical truth must remain intact even if infrastructure fails, actors are compromised, or worlds become hostile.

2. Governance Must Prevail

Governance authority must remain functional and authoritative under all conditions.

3. Chains Must Endure

Provenance, lineage, custody, and governance chains must remain reconstructable and verifiable.

4. Recovery Must Be Deterministic

Recovery must produce the same canonical truth regardless of environment, infrastructure, or attack conditions.

Resilience is constitutional, not optional.

6.8.3 Continuity Under Attack

Berry must remain operational under:

sustained adversarial probing

partial system compromise

world‑level corruption

key compromise events

infrastructure outages

network partitioning

governance escalation storms

chain reconstruction events

Continuity does not mean “everything works.”Continuity means:

truth remains intact

governance remains authoritative

enforcement remains functional

canonical data remains verifiable

Everything else is secondary.

6.8.4 Canonical Truth Resilience

Canonical truth must remain:

reconstructable

verifiable

tamper‑evident

cryptographically anchored

independent of runtime state

independent of world‑context

Berry guarantees that:

chain reconstruction can always restore truth

integrityHash can always be recomputed

canonical state can always be validated

corrupted state can always be isolated

Truth must outlive infrastructure.

6.8.5 Governance Resilience

Governance must remain functional even under:

compromised actors

compromised keys

compromised worlds

compromised infrastructure

Governance resilience requires:

multi‑actor authority

key revocation procedures

emergency governance modes

immutable governance logs

tamper‑evident rulings

Governance is the final line of defense — it must never fail.

6.8.6 Chain Resilience

Provenance, lineage, custody, and governance chains must remain:

append‑only

hash‑linked

signature‑verified

reconstructable from first principles

Even if:

storage is corrupted

nodes are compromised

worlds are hostile

partial data is lost

Berry must be able to rebuild chains deterministically.

6.8.7 Infrastructure Resilience

Infrastructure resilience requires:

isolation boundaries

redundant verification paths

immutable canonical storage

deterministic execution

fallback verification logic

environment‑independent reconstruction

Infrastructure may fail.Resilience ensures truth does not.

6.8.8 Attack Resilience

Berry must remain resilient against:

coordinated multi‑vector attacks

insider threats

world‑context poisoning

governance impersonation

chain disruption attempts

cryptographic manipulation

system‑level tampering

Resilience is measured not by how few attacks succeed, but by how little damage they can cause.

6.8.9 Recovery Resilience

Recovery must:

restore canonical truth

re‑establish chain continuity

rotate compromised keys

re‑isolate compromised worlds

re‑validate governance authority

harden affected systems

Recovery must be:

deterministic

cryptographically verifiable

governance‑authorized

tamper‑evident

Recovery is not a patch — it is a constitutional process.

6.8.10 Institutional Continuity

Berry must remain an institution even under existential threat.

Institutional continuity requires:

truth that cannot be erased

governance that cannot be impersonated

chains that cannot be rewritten

authority that cannot be fabricated

recovery that cannot be subverted

Berry must outlive:

infrastructure

actors

worlds

attacks

Institutions endure.Berry must endure.

6.8.11 Constitutional Principle

Security protects integrity.Integrity protects truth.Resilience protects everything.
