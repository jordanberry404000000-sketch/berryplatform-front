9.1 Public Logs & Access Tiers

9.1.1 Purpose

Public Logs & Access Tiers define what Berry logs, how it logs it, and who can see what.This chapter ensures that:

all institutional actions are recorded

logs are immutable and tamper‑evident

visibility matches responsibility

users can inspect what affects them

governance cannot hide behind opacity

Logs are the backbone of transparency.

9.1.2 Logging Philosophy

Berry’s logging model is built on three principles:

1. Everything That Matters Is Logged

If it affects users, worlds, authority, or fairness — it is recorded.

2. Logs Must Be Immutable

Once written, logs cannot be altered, deleted, or rewritten.

3. Logs Must Be Accessible

Visibility is tiered, but nothing is hidden from the institution.

Logging is not optional — it is constitutional.

9.1.3 What Berry Logs

Berry logs:

governance decisions

enforcement actions

dispute and appeal submissions

dispute and appeal outcomes

evidence capture events

authority use

world‑level operations

system‑level corrections

precedent creation

institutional learning updates

If it shapes the institution, it is logged.

9.1.4 Log Structure

Every log entry must include:

log identifier

event type

actor or world involved

authority snapshot

timestamps

evidence references

chain state

world‑context

cryptographic signatures

impact summary

Logs must be complete, consistent, and auditable.

9.1.5 Transparency Access Tiers

Berry uses a four‑tier access model:

Tier 1 — Public Logs

Visible to all users.Includes:

governance summaries

dispute outcomes

precedent summaries

institutional reports

Tier 2 — Actor Logs

Visible to actors involved in a process.Includes:

evidence relevant to their dispute

enforcement actions affecting them

procedural steps

Tier 3 — System Logs

Visible to enforcement and system authorities.Includes:

cross‑world evidence

authority use

structural corrections

Tier 4 — Governance Logs

Visible only to governance.Includes:

full evidence sets

constitutional interpretations

internal consistency checks

Access increases with responsibility.

9.1.6 Log Integrity Requirements

All logs must be:

hash‑linked

cryptographically signed

globally consistent

redundantly stored

immutable

timestamp‑verified

If a log fails integrity checks, it is invalid.

9.1.7 Logging Failure Modes

Berry must detect and prevent:

missing logs

forged logs

suppressed logs

altered logs

contradictory logs

selective logging

logging bypasses

Any attempt to interfere with logging is a constitutional violation.

9.1.8 Constitutional Principle

Logs are the institution’s memory.Access is the institution’s conscience.Together, they create trust.
