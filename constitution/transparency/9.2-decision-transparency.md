9.2.1 Purpose

Decision Transparency defines how Berry explains the decisions it makes, including governance rulings, enforcement actions, dispute outcomes, and systemic corrections.This chapter ensures that:

decisions are understandable

decisions are traceable

decisions are justified

decisions cannot be hidden

users know why something happened, not just what happened

Transparency without explanation is noise.

9.2.2 Decision Types Covered

Decision Transparency applies to:

governance decisions

enforcement actions

dispute resolutions

appeal outcomes

authority adjustments

systemic corrections

precedent creation

institutional learning updates

If it affects users or worlds, it must be explained.

9.2.3 Decision Explanation Requirements

Every decision must include:

decision identifier

decision type

summary of the action taken

justification

evidence references

constitutional references (if applicable)

authority snapshot

timestamps

world‑context

impact assessment

Decisions must be complete, clear, and reproducible.

9.2.4 Explanation Standards

Berry applies three standards when explaining decisions:

1. Clarity Standard

“Can a reasonable actor understand this decision?”

2. Traceability Standard

“Can the decision be traced back to evidence and constitutional principles?”

3. Consistency Standard

“Does the explanation align with precedent and institutional behavior?”

Explanations must be written for humans, not machines.

9.2.5 Public Decision Summaries

Berry publishes public summaries for:

governance rulings

dispute outcomes

appeal outcomes

precedent creation

institutional corrections

Summaries must be:

accurate

non‑technical

accessible

free of sensitive data

Public summaries build public trust.

9.2.6 Actor‑Level Decision Detail

Actors directly affected by a decision receive:

full evidence references

procedural breakdown

authority chain

justification

next steps

appeal options

Actors deserve full clarity about decisions that affect them.

9.2.7 Decision Transparency Failure Modes

Berry must detect and prevent:

hidden decisions

incomplete explanations

contradictory explanations

suppressed explanations

forged explanations

selective transparency

explanation bypasses

Any attempt to obscure decision‑making is a constitutional violation.

9.2.8 Constitutional Principle

A decision without explanation is power.A decision with explanation is governance.Berry chooses governance.
