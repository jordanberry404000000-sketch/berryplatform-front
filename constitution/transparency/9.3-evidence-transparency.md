9.3 Evidence Transparency

9.3.1 Purpose

Evidence Transparency defines how Berry exposes evidence to the correct parties while protecting privacy, integrity, and fairness.This chapter ensures that:

evidence is visible to those who need it

evidence cannot be hidden or selectively revealed

evidence is protected from tampering

transparency does not compromise safety

users understand how evidence shaped decisions

Evidence is the foundation of trust — but only when handled responsibly.

9.3.2 Evidence Visibility Principles

Berry’s evidence transparency model is built on three principles:

1. Relevant Actors See Relevant Evidence

Actors involved in a dispute or appeal must see the evidence used.

2. Sensitive Evidence Is Tier‑Controlled

Some evidence requires restricted access to protect privacy or security.

3. Governance Sees Everything

Governance must have full visibility to ensure fairness and consistency.

Transparency must never compromise safety.

9.3.3 Evidence Transparency Tiers

Berry uses a structured tier system:

Tier 1 — Public Evidence Summaries

Visible to all users.Includes:

high‑level descriptions

non‑sensitive context

institutional patterns

Tier 2 — Actor Evidence Access

Visible to actors involved in a dispute.Includes:

full evidence relevant to their case

procedural logs

authority chain

Tier 3 — System Evidence Access

Visible to enforcement and system authorities.Includes:

cross‑world evidence

structural evidence

authority use patterns

Tier 4 — Governance Evidence Access

Visible only to governance.Includes:

full evidence sets

sensitive data

constitutional impact analysis

Evidence visibility increases with responsibility.

9.3.4 Evidence Explanation Requirements

Whenever evidence is used in a decision, Berry must provide:

a summary of the evidence

how it was captured

how it was verified

how it influenced the decision

any limitations or uncertainties

Evidence must be understandable, not just available.

9.3.5 Evidence Integrity Requirements

All evidence must be:

hash‑linked

cryptographically signed

timestamp‑verified

immutable

redundantly stored

globally consistent

If evidence fails integrity checks, it is invalid.

9.3.6 Evidence Transparency Failure Modes

Berry must detect and prevent:

hidden evidence

selectively revealed evidence

forged evidence

suppressed evidence

contradictory evidence

evidence tampering

evidence access bypasses

Any attempt to interfere with evidence transparency is a constitutional violation.

9.3.7 Constitutional Principle

Evidence is truth.Transparency is trust.Berry protects both.
