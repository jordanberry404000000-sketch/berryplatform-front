9.4.1 Purpose

Institutional Reporting defines how Berry publishes periodic reports about its decisions, disputes, enforcement actions, precedent, and overall institutional health.This chapter ensures that:

the institution is accountable to the public

patterns and trends are visible

governance behavior is inspectable

systemic issues cannot hide

users understand how the institution is evolving

Reporting is the institution speaking openly to the world.

9.4.2 Reporting Philosophy

Berry’s reporting model is built on three principles:

1. Regularity Creates Trust

Reports must be predictable, consistent, and timely.

2. Honesty Creates Legitimacy

Reports must reflect reality — not marketing.

3. Insight Creates Understanding

Reports must help users understand institutional behavior.

Reports are not PR. They are accountability.

9.4.3 Reporting Cadence

Berry publishes:

Monthly Reports

Operational summaries, dispute statistics, enforcement metrics.

Quarterly Reports

Governance activity, precedent updates, systemic corrections.

Annual Reports

Institutional health, long‑term trends, constitutional impact.

Cadence must be consistent across all worlds.

9.4.4 Report Contents

Each report must include:

dispute volume and outcomes

appeal volume and outcomes

enforcement actions

authority use patterns

precedent created

systemic corrections

evidence integrity metrics

transparency metrics

institutional learning updates

cross‑world consistency checks

Reports must be complete, accurate, and auditable.

9.4.5 Public vs Tiered Reporting

Berry uses a tiered reporting model:

Public Reports

High‑level summaries, trends, and institutional insights.

Actor Reports

Case‑specific summaries for involved actors.

System Reports

Operational metrics for enforcement and system authorities.

Governance Reports

Full institutional analysis, including sensitive data.

Reporting visibility increases with responsibility.

9.4.6 Reporting Integrity Requirements

All reports must be:

hash‑linked

cryptographically signed

timestamp‑verified

immutable

globally consistent

publicly accessible (for public tiers)

If a report fails integrity checks, it is invalid.

9.4.7 Reporting Failure Modes

Berry must detect and prevent:

missing reports

delayed reports

forged reports

selectively edited reports

suppressed reports

contradictory reports

reporting bypasses

Any attempt to interfere with reporting is a constitutional violation.

9.4.8 Constitutional Principle

Institutions that report are accountable.Institutions that hide are corrupt.Berry reports. Always.
