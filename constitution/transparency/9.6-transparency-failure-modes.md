9.6.1 Purpose

Transparency Failure Modes define how Berry detects, responds to, and recovers from failures in transparency, including missing logs, hidden decisions, suppressed evidence, or inconsistent reporting.This chapter ensures that:

transparency cannot silently degrade

failures are detected immediately

failures trigger automatic escalation

the institution cannot hide its own mistakes

recovery is structured, not improvised

Transparency is only meaningful if its failures are impossible to ignore.

9.6.2 What Constitutes a Transparency Failure

A transparency failure occurs when:

a required log is missing

a log is altered or inconsistent

evidence is hidden or selectively revealed

a decision lacks explanation

a report is missing or delayed

monitoring alerts are suppressed

transparency tiers are bypassed

governance visibility is obstructed

If transparency is weakened, the institution is weakened.

9.6.3 Failure Detection Mechanisms

Berry must automatically detect:

1. Log Gaps

Missing, suppressed, or contradictory logs.

2. Evidence Visibility Gaps

Evidence not visible to the correct tier.

3. Decision Explanation Gaps

Decisions lacking justification or traceability.

4. Reporting Gaps

Missing, late, or inconsistent reports.

5. Monitoring Gaps

Suppressed alerts or silent failures.

6. Cross‑World Divergence

Transparency inconsistencies between worlds.

Detection must be continuous and deterministic.

9.6.4 Automatic Response Protocol

When a transparency failure is detected, Berry must:

generate a transparency‑failure alert

classify severity

notify the appropriate tier

log the failure

trigger automatic review

escalate to governance (if severe)

initiate corrective action

Failures cannot be hidden or ignored.

9.6.5 Corrective Actions

Corrective actions may include:

reconstructing missing logs

restoring evidence visibility

issuing corrected decision explanations

publishing delayed reports

re‑running integrity checks

initiating systemic corrections

updating precedent or procedures

Corrective actions must be logged and auditable.

9.6.6 Transparency Failure Severity Levels

Berry classifies failures into:

Level 1 — Minor

Non‑critical inconsistencies; corrected automatically.

Level 2 — Moderate

Affects actor visibility; requires governance review.

Level 3 — Severe

Affects institutional trust; triggers immediate governance escalation.

Level 4 — Critical

Threatens constitutional integrity; triggers emergency protocols.

Severity determines escalation.

9.6.7 Transparency Failure Modes to Prevent

Berry must detect and prevent:

hidden failures

forged transparency records

selective transparency

suppressed alerts

inconsistent transparency across worlds

transparency bypasses

silent degradation

Any attempt to interfere with transparency is a constitutional violation.

9.6.8 Constitutional Principle

Transparency is only real when its failures are visible.Berry exposes failures so trust never collapses.
