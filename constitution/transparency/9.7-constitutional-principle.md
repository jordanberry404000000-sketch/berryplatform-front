9.7 Transparency Constitutional Principle

Transparency is the foundation of institutional trust.Without visibility, fairness collapses.Without accountability, power corrupts.Berry exists to ensure neither ever happens.

Berry guarantees that:

all meaningful actions are visible

all decisions are explainable

all evidence is inspectable

all logs are immutable

all failures are exposed

all authorities are accountable

all worlds receive consistent truth

Transparency is not a feature.It is a constitutional right.

Berry does not ask for trust —it earns it through visibility.
